Where We Are

How the Program Works

Every session fields four courts, ranked top to bottom, run as a liquid ladder. Rosters are built by position, not overall rank. A Week 1 baseline test seeds a positional depth chart (setters, outsides, and so on), and each court is assembled top-down so it's a playable team. You advance by beating the player above you at your position.

  • The 1-2 and 3-4 boundaries move weekly, earned off the standards board and Wednesday league results.
  • The 2-3 boundary moves at phase checkpoints (after weeks 2, 4, and 6), with a guaranteed minimum number of players moving up each time.
  • Swing players, the top few on court 3, train with courts 1-2 on Mondays and play their home court on Wednesdays.
  • Riser call-ups: each Wednesday, one or two standouts from courts 3-4 play league night up a court.
  • Placement is private (each player knows her own court and what she's chasing) but the pathway is public: posted standards, weekly rosters, and celebrated promotions.

Demo-disperse teaching: all four courts run the identical session card, scaled by level. New material is taught once, centrally, by the fall ball lead, with the whole gym watching (a 4-minute cap, twice a night), then courts disperse and run it: same drill names and cue words everywhere, just different numbers for entry difficulty, tempo, and reps.

One gym clock, one whistle: balls in carts, 90-second transitions, no water until the whistle says so.

A Typical Practice Night

Every session is 105 minutes. Monday is teach night (new material); Wednesday is league night (more match play, lighter teaching).

Monday: Teach Night

0:00

Warm-up

Gym-wide dynamic warm-up, one leader at center court.

0:08

One-Arm Series

Daily ball-control constant on home courts.

0:20

Serve + Serve-Receive

Half the court serves at targets, half passes live serves; groups swap partway through.

0:37

Demo 1 + Teach Block

Central 4-minute demo at court 1, then all four courts run the new material at their own level.

1:02

Demo 2 + System Drill

Second short demo, then the week's system drill on every court.

1:37

Competitive Closer + Close

A quick scored game, then points, promotions, and a look ahead to Wednesday.

Wednesday: League Night

0:00

Warm-up + Constants

Same warm-up, one-arm series, and serve/serve-receive block as Monday.

0:37

Quick Refresher + Application

A short refresher (or none) on Monday's material under game conditions.

1:02

League Play

38 protected minutes of league matches. This block never gets shortened.

1:40

Close

League table update and riser call-up announcements.

Per-player load stays capped at roughly 40 to 50 quality serves and 40 to 60 full-approach swings a session.

The Fall League

Match play is tiered so it's competitive at every level: a performance league (4 squads, courts 1-2) and a development league (4 squads, courts 3-4), each running its own standings table.

Squads are drawn from the whole pool, not just one court, and built by a positional draft so every squad has a setter, a few pins, a middle, and a defender. Squads are redrafted at every phase checkpoint, so the league genuinely changes shape as the ladder moves.

League formats rotate by phase: standards races early, then wash games and tournaments, then Spanish Six / Arkansas / 5-4-3-2-1 brackets for the finale stretch.

The standards leaderboard is gym-wide and pool-blind: perfect-pass counts, tape flips, talk counts, and improvement points go on one board for all 60 players. A development-league player can lead the entire gym, and several will.
